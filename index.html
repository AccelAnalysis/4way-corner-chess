How it plays

  • Capture a king to eliminate that player.
  • The capturer takes control of the eliminated army.
  • Pawns lock direction after their first move.
  • The last player with a king wins.

Rules

Capture kings. Command corners.

Four players begin in the four corners. Pieces move like standard chess pieces, except pawns are adapted for a four-direction board.

  • Objective: be the last active player with a king.
  • King capture: kings are captured directly; there is no check or checkmate layer in this prototype.
  • Takeover: when you capture a king, the eliminated player’s remaining pieces become yours.
  • Pawns: a pawn’s first move can be one square in any direction. That direction then locks. Later, it advances in that direction and captures one square diagonally or side-forward relative to that direction.
  • AI: easy plays randomly, medium prefers valuable captures, and hard also values king pressure and army strength.
